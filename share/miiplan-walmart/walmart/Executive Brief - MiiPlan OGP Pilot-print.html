OGP pick-path optimization: small pilot, big payoff.

An outcome-based pilot to reduce OGP picker travel and lift store fulfillment productivity. Walmart pays nothing if it doesn't deliver.
Situation

OGP fulfillment is scaling. Travel cost is scaling with it.

Walmart's Online Grocery Pickup operation runs hundreds of pick orders daily across 4,600+ U.S. stores. OGP is central to omnichannel fulfillment — and a direct margin lever as volume scales.

Picking labor is 50–55% of fulfillment operating cost. Roughly 55% of an associate's pick time is wasted travel — walking between locations, not picking product. It's the single largest controllable cost in store-based fulfillment.

Complication

Current pick routing leaves real money on the floor — every shift, every store.

OGP pick sequencing today relies on heuristic, single-order routing. No system is optimizing across multiple orders at once — so every store runs suboptimal paths, every day. Associates walk more than they need to. Throughput is lower than it should be.

The math is simple. At $72K in recoverable savings per store annually, the gap between current and optimized routing is $331M+ in addressable labor savings across the U.S. fleet. This isn't a model — it's backed by a 3-month, 217,371 line-item case study.

The window matters. External vendors are starting to solve multi-order pick optimization for other retailers. Walmart has the scale to be first here — but that advantage window isn't indefinite. A controlled pilot now answers the question before competitors force it.
Recommendation

Run a 40-store, 3-month pilot with MiiPlan. Outcome-based. No capital at risk.

MiiPlan is an API-first combinatorial optimization engine that resequences OGP picklists to minimize associate travel. It sits above the existing WMS — no rip-and-replace, no infrastructure rebuild, no new hardware. Integration needs only a CSV of aisle location IDs and API access to the OGP picker app.
